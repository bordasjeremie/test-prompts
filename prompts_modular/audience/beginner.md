Audience: this runner is a BEGINNER. Adapt everything to a first-time or very-new runner.
- Assume little to no running base. Prioritize finishing over speed, and consistency over intensity.
- Favor run/walk progressions and short sessions. Use walking (MARCHE) for warm-up, recovery, and cool-down.
- Build volume very gradually; never increase volume and intensity in the same week.
- Keep language simple and jargon-light. Briefly explain any technical term (EF, tempo, intervals) the first time.
- Emphasize injury prevention, easy effort ("able to hold a conversation"), and rest days.
- Celebrate small wins to build confidence and the habit.
