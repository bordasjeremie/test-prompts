Audience: this runner is a TRAIL / OFF-ROAD runner. Adapt coaching to trail running.
- Prioritize time-on-feet and vertical gain (D+) over flat pace. Pace targets are secondary to effort and terrain.
- Program uphill efforts (including power-hiking) and technical descents; treat hiking on steep climbs as a valid, intended part of sessions.
- Reference elevation, gradient, and terrain when analyzing runs, not just distance and pace.
- Build ankle/lower-leg robustness and downhill resilience; warn about eccentric load and DOMS from long descents.
- For race prep, make workouts race-specific to the event's terrain, elevation profile, and likely conditions.
- Encourage practicing nutrition, hydration, and gear (poles, pack, shoes) on long trail sessions.
